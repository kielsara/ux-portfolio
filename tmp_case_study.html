UX Audit & Redesign

Turning 7 months of heuristic analysis into actionable design change.

I led a comprehensive UX audit of Synchrony Business Solutions' analytics platform — a tool used by enterprise clients to track performance, spending, and business insights. What started as a usability review became a 7-month deep dive into data visualization clarity, accessibility, and design system foundations.

The Challenge

An analytics platform that had grown faster than its design.

The Synchrony Business Solutions analytics platform serves enterprise clients making critical business decisions. But years of feature additions without systematic design review had created inconsistencies — confusing data visualizations, accessibility gaps, and a fragmented user experience. Leadership knew something was wrong, but needed clear evidence and a path forward.

Audit Approach

Systematic evaluation, not subjective opinion.

I structured the audit around established frameworks — Nielsen Norman Group heuristics, Laws of UX, WCAG accessibility guidelines, and data visualization best practices. This gave stakeholders confidence that findings were grounded in research, not personal preference.

01
Framework Selection
Chose evaluation criteria spanning usability heuristics, accessibility, data viz clarity, and content consistency.

Documented every screen with observations, severity ratings, and evidence-based recommendations.

04
Pattern Identification

What I Found

The problems weren't random — they were patterns.

What initially looked like scattered usability issues revealed deeper systemic problems. The same mistakes repeated across screens, suggesting gaps in design guidelines rather than one-off errors.

📊
Data Visualization Clarity
Charts lacked clear labels, used inconsistent color coding, and buried critical insights in visual noise.
♿
Accessibility Gaps
Color contrast failures, missing alt text, keyboard navigation issues — blocking users with disabilities.
🧭
Navigation Inconsistency
Users couldn't predict where they'd land. Same actions behaved differently across sections.
📝
Content & Labeling
Jargon-heavy labels, inconsistent terminology, and missing context left users guessing.
Making Sense of It

From 100+ sticky notes to 6 actionable themes.

I clustered findings using affinity mapping in FigJam, grouping raw observations into themes that could drive design decisions. This transformed an overwhelming spreadsheet of issues into a clear story stakeholders could act on.

Getting Buy-In

Audit findings don't matter if nobody acts on them.

I presented findings in a structured design session with cross-functional stakeholders — product managers, developers, and senior designers. The goal wasn't just to share problems, but to build shared ownership of solutions. I prioritized issues by severity and effort, giving the team a clear roadmap.

“This gave us the evidence we needed to prioritize accessibility work that had been deprioritized for years.”

— Senior Product Designer, Synchrony
The Redesign

Translating critique into craft.

I didn't stop at identifying problems. Working with the UX team, I developed design recommendations that addressed root causes — contributing to a variable design system that would prevent these issues from recurring.

Chart Redesign Standards

Created guidelines for data visualization clarity — consistent color palettes, clear axis labels, and progressive disclosure for complex datasets.

Accessibility Patterns

Documented accessible component patterns with proper contrast ratios, focus states, and screen reader support.

Navigation Framework

Proposed consistent navigation patterns that users could learn once and apply everywhere.

Reflection

What 7 months of auditing taught me about design leadership.

This project pushed me beyond pixel-level thinking into systems thinking. I learned that identifying problems is only half the work — the harder part is building consensus and creating documentation that outlives your tenure.

  • Evidence beats opinion. Grounding critique in heuristics gave my recommendations credibility with senior stakeholders.
  • Scope discipline matters. I had to resist the urge to fix everything and focus on high-impact, achievable changes.
  • Documentation is design. The guidelines I created will shape decisions long after my internship ended.
  • Collaboration amplifies impact. Working with 6 evaluators caught blind spots I would have missed alone.
